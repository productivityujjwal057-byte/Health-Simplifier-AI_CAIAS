📋 All Cardiac Tests — What They Detect

        


          

            

              ⚡
              

                
ECG / EKG

                
Electrocardiogram

              

              ▼
            

            

              
Measures electrical activity of the heart. Records the rhythm, rate, and pattern of every heartbeat using electrodes placed on skin.

              
Detects:

              
    
                
  • 🔴 Irregular heartbeat (arrhythmia) — AFib, VT, bradycardia
    • 
                
  • 🔴 Heart attack (STEMI/NSTEMI) — ST elevation or depression
    • 
                
  • 🟡 Left ventricular hypertrophy (LVH)
    • 
                
  • 🟡 Bundle branch blocks (LBBB/RBBB)
    • 
                
  • 🟡 QT prolongation — sudden death risk
    • 
                
  • ✅ Normal sinus rhythm — healthy electrical pattern
    • 
              

              
💡 First test done when heart disease is suspected. Takes 5 minutes, painless.

            

          


          

            

              🔊
              

                
Echocardiogram

                
Echo — Ultrasound of the Heart

              

              ▼
            

            

              
Uses ultrasound waves to create real-time images of the beating heart. Shows structure, movement, and pumping function.

              
Detects:

              
    
                
  • 🔴 Heart failure — low ejection fraction (EF <40%)
    • 
                
  • 🔴 Valve disease — stenosis (narrowing) or regurgitation (leaking)
    • 
                
  • 🔴 Cardiomyopathy — enlarged or thickened heart muscle
    • 
                
  • 🟡 Pericardial effusion — fluid around heart
    • 
                
  • 🟡 Wall motion abnormalities after heart attack
    • 
                
  • ✅ Normal EF 55–70% means good pumping function
    • 
              

              
💡 Key number to know: Ejection Fraction (EF). Normal is 55–70%. Below 40% is heart failure.

            

          


          

            

              🩸
              

                
Blood Pressure Test

                
Sphygmomanometry

              

              ▼
            

            

              
Measures the force of blood pushing against artery walls. Reported as systolic/diastolic (e.g., 120/80 mmHg).

              
Ranges:

              
    
                
  • ✅ Normal: <120/80 mmHg
    • 
                
  • 🟡 Elevated: 120–129 / <80 mmHg — lifestyle change needed
    • 
                
  • 🟡 Stage 1 Hypertension: 130–139 / 80–89 mmHg
    • 
                
  • 🔴 Stage 2 Hypertension: ≥140/90 mmHg — medication needed
    • 
                
  • 🔴 Hypertensive Crisis: >180/120 — emergency!
    • 
                
  • 🟡 Low BP (Hypotension): <90/60 — dizziness, fainting
    • 
              

              
💡 High BP is the #1 cause of stroke and heart attack. Called the "silent killer" — no symptoms until damage is done.

            

          


          

            

              🧪
              

                
Lipid Profile Test

                
Blood Cholesterol Test

              

              ▼
            

            

              
Blood test measuring all forms of fat (lipids) in the blood. Done fasting for 9–12 hours. Most important cardiac risk assessment test.

              
Normal ranges:

              
    
                
  • ✅ Total Cholesterol: <200 mg/dL (optimal)
    • 
                
  • 🔴 LDL (bad): <100 mg/dL optimal; >160 high risk
    • 
                
  • ✅ HDL (good): >60 mg/dL protective; <40 danger
    • 
                
  • 🟡 Triglycerides: <150 normal; >200 high risk
    • 
                
  • 🔴 High LDL + low HDL = major heart attack risk
    • 
              

              
💡 Even a 10% reduction in LDL reduces heart attack risk by 20–25%. Statins are highly effective.

            

          


          

            

              💉
              

                
Troponin Test

                
Cardiac Troponin I / T

              

              ▼
            

            

              
Blood test that detects troponin — a protein released when heart muscle cells are damaged or dying. Gold standard for heart attack diagnosis.

              
Interpretation:

              
    
                
  • ✅ Normal: Troponin I <0.04 ng/mL (lab-specific ranges vary)
    • 
                
  • 🔴 Elevated: Confirms myocardial injury — heart attack likely
    • 
                
  • 🔴 Rising trend (serial tests 3–6 hours apart) = heart attack in progress
    • 
                
  • 🟡 Mildly elevated: Can also indicate myocarditis, PE, or severe infection
    • 
                
  • 💡 High-sensitivity troponin (hsTnI) detects damage within 1 hour
    • 
              

              
💡 Most important test in Emergency Room for chest pain. 99% sensitive for heart attack when tested at 0 and 3 hours.

            

          


          

            

              🏃
              

                
Stress Test (TMT)

                
Treadmill / Exercise Stress Test

              

              ▼
            

            

              
ECG recorded while patient walks on a treadmill at increasing speeds. The heart is stressed to reveal problems not visible at rest.

              
Detects:

              
    
                
  • 🔴 Blocked coronary arteries — causes ST depression during exercise
    • 
                
  • 🔴 Exercise-induced arrhythmias — dangerous irregular rhythm
    • 
                
  • 🟡 Poor exercise tolerance — indicates reduced heart reserve
    • 
                
  • 🟡 Abnormal blood pressure response during exercise
    • 
                
  • ✅ Negative test = lower risk of significant coronary disease
    • 
              

              
💡 If TMT is positive (ST depression >2mm), coronary angiography is usually the next step.

            

          


          

            

              💉
              

                
Coronary Angiography

                
Cardiac Catheterization

              

              ▼
            

            

              
Invasive procedure: dye injected into coronary arteries via catheter. X-ray images show exact location and severity of blockages. Gold standard for coronary artery disease.

              
Detects:

              
    
                
  • 🔴 Coronary artery stenosis — percentage of blockage in each artery
    • 
                
  • 🔴 Total occlusion — 100% blocked artery requiring urgent stent
    • 
                
  • 🟡 Collateral circulation — natural bypass vessels
    • 
                
  • 💊 During procedure: angioplasty + stent can be placed immediately
    • 
                
  • 🔴 Significant stenosis >70% usually requires stent or bypass surgery
    • 
              

              
💡 Both diagnostic and potentially therapeutic — a blockage found can often be treated in the same procedure.

            

          


          

            

              🖥️
              

                
Cardiac CT Scan

                
CT Coronary Angiography / Calcium Score

              

              ▼
            

            

              
Non-invasive CT imaging of the heart and coronary arteries. Can calculate Coronary Artery Calcium (CAC) score and show arterial blockages without catheter.

              
Detects:

              
    
                
  • 🔴 Calcium deposits in arteries — CAC score >400 = high risk
    • 
                
  • 🔴 Coronary artery stenosis — non-invasive blockage detection
    • 
                
  • 🟡 Plaque (soft and hard) in arterial walls
    • 
                
  • ✅ CAC score = 0 means very low 10-year cardiac risk
    • 
                
  • 🟡 Congenital heart anomalies and structural defects
    • 
              

              
💡 CAC score is one of the best predictors of future heart attack risk. Score 0 is very reassuring.

            

          


          

            

              🧲
              

                
Cardiac MRI

                
Magnetic Resonance Imaging — Heart

              

              ▼
            

            

              
Advanced imaging using magnetic fields and radio waves. Provides the most detailed pictures of heart tissue, function, and blood flow without radiation.

              
Detects:

              
    
                
  • 🔴 Myocardial scarring — scar tissue from old heart attacks (late gadolinium enhancement)
    • 
                
  • 🔴 Myocarditis — inflammation of heart muscle
    • 
                
  • 🔴 Cardiomyopathy — precise measurement of heart dimensions
    • 
                
  • 🟡 Pericardial disease — pericarditis, effusion, constriction
    • 
                
  • ✅ Most accurate measurement of ejection fraction (EF)
    • 
              

              
💡 Gold standard for cardiomyopathy and myocarditis diagnosis. No radiation exposure unlike CT.

📋 All Neurological Tests — What They Detect

        


          

            

              🖥️
              

                
CT Scan (Brain)

                
Computed Tomography — Head

              

              ▼
            

            

              
Uses X-rays and computer processing to create cross-sectional images of the brain. Fast test (5 minutes) — first done in emergencies.

              
Detects:

              
    
                
  • 🔴 Brain hemorrhage — bleeding inside skull (most sensitive CT finding)
    • 
                
  • 🔴 Acute stroke — large ischemic area, hemorrhagic transformation
    • 
                
  • 🔴 Brain injury — contusion, fractures, midline shift
    • 
                
  • 🟡 Brain tumors — mass lesions, calcifications
    • 
                
  • 🟡 Hydrocephalus — enlarged fluid spaces
    • 
                
  • ✅ First test in any head injury or sudden severe headache
    • 
              

              
💡 CT is the fastest and most accessible brain scan. MRI is more detailed but takes longer.

            

          


          

            

              🧲
              

                
MRI (Brain)

                
Magnetic Resonance Imaging — Brain

              

              ▼
            

            

              
Uses magnetic fields and radio waves to create detailed images of brain tissue. Far more sensitive than CT for most brain conditions. No radiation.

              
Detects:

              
    
                
  • 🔴 Brain tumors — size, location, spread, type (glioma vs meningioma)
    • 
                
  • 🔴 Ischemic stroke — DWI (diffusion) detects within 30 minutes
    • 
                
  • 🔴 Multiple sclerosis — white matter lesions (plaques)
    • 
                
  • 🟡 Alzheimer's — hippocampal atrophy, cortical thinning
    • 
                
  • 🟡 Epilepsy focus — structural cause of seizures
    • 
                
  • 🟡 Aneurysm — MRA (MRI angiography) shows vascular abnormalities
    • 
              

              
💡 Gold standard for most brain conditions. DWI sequence is critical in acute stroke — detects infarct within minutes of onset.

            

          


          

            

              ⚡
              

                
EEG

                
Electroencephalogram

              

              ▼
            

            

              
Records electrical activity of the brain using electrodes placed on the scalp. The "ECG for the brain." Can record during sleep for overnight monitoring.

              
Detects:

              
    
                
  • 🔴 Epilepsy — interictal spikes, sharp waves, seizure discharges
    • 
                
  • 🔴 Status epilepticus — continuous seizure activity
    • 
                
  • 🟡 Encephalopathy — diffuse slowing from metabolic causes
    • 
                
  • 🟡 Sleep disorders — sleep staging, REM behavior disorder
    • 
                
  • 🟡 Brain death — electrocerebral silence (flat EEG)
    • 
                
  • ✅ Normal EEG does NOT rule out epilepsy (40% of epileptics have normal EEG)
    • 
              

              
💡 A 24–72 hour ambulatory EEG is much more sensitive than a routine 20-minute EEG for capturing seizures.

            

          


          

            

              🔆
              

                
PET Scan (Brain)

                
Positron Emission Tomography

              

              ▼
            

            

              
Functional imaging — shows metabolic activity of brain regions using radioactive tracer (usually FDG-glucose). Areas with low activity appear dark.

              
Detects:

              
    
                
  • 🔴 Alzheimer's disease — hypometabolism in temporal/parietal lobes
    • 
                
  • 🔴 Amyloid PET — directly detects amyloid plaques (Alzheimer's hallmark)
    • 
                
  • 🔴 Brain tumor activity vs radiation necrosis — key treatment decision
    • 
                
  • 🟡 Parkinson's — dopamine transporter (DaT) scan shows nigral degeneration
    • 
                
  • 🟡 Epilepsy focus localization before surgery
    • 
              

              
💡 Amyloid PET (2024) can detect Alzheimer's 10–15 years before symptoms appear — enabling early intervention.

            

          


          

            

              💉
              

                
Lumbar Puncture

                
Spinal Tap — CSF Analysis

              

              ▼
            

            

              
Needle inserted into lower spine to collect cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) — the fluid surrounding brain and spinal cord. Analyzed for cells, proteins, glucose, and pathogens.

              
Detects:

              
    
                
  • 🔴 Bacterial meningitis — turbid CSF, high WBC, low glucose, bacteria on culture
    • 
                
  • 🔴 Viral meningitis / encephalitis — lymphocytic pleocytosis
    • 
                
  • 🔴 Subarachnoid hemorrhage — xanthochromia (yellow CSF)
    • 
                
  • 🟡 Multiple sclerosis — oligoclonal bands in CSF
    • 
                
  • 🟡 Alzheimer's — low Aβ42, high tau in CSF
    • 
              

              
💡 Always done AFTER CT scan to rule out raised intracranial pressure (blind LP in raised ICP can cause brain herniation).

            

          


          

            

              🩹
              

                
Brain Angiography

                
Cerebral / Brain Angiogram

              

              ▼
            

            

              
Uses dye injected into brain arteries to create detailed X-ray images of blood vessels. Gold standard for cerebrovascular disease. Can be invasive (DSA) or non-invasive (CTA/MRA).

              
Detects:

              
    
                
  • 🔴 Intracranial aneurysm — ballooning of artery wall, rupture risk
    • 
                
  • 🔴 AVM (Arteriovenous Malformation) — abnormal tangle of vessels
    • 
                
  • 🔴 Carotid artery stenosis — narrowing causing stroke/TIA risk
    • 
                
  • 🟡 Vasospasm after subarachnoid hemorrhage
    • 
                
  • 🔴 Acute ischemic stroke — clot visible in major artery (for thrombectomy)
    • 
              

              
💡 DSA (digital subtraction angiography) remains gold standard. Endovascular treatment (coiling, stenting) can be done in same procedure.

            

          


          

            

              👨‍⚕️
              

                
Neurological Examination

                
Clinical Neuro Exam

              

              ▼
            

            

              
Bedside physical examination performed by neurologist. Tests every part of the nervous system systematically. Often reveals diagnosis before any scan is needed.

              
Checks:

              
    
                
  • 🧠 Mental status — MMSE/MoCA for memory and cognition
    • 
                
  • 👁️ Cranial nerves — vision, eye movement, facial sensation
    • 
                
  • 💪 Motor function — power, tone, coordination in all limbs
    • 
                
  • 🖐️ Sensory function — pain, temperature, vibration, proprioception
    • 
                
  • 🔨 Reflexes — hyperreflexia (UMN), hyporeflexia (LMN), Babinski sign
    • 
                
  • 🚶 Gait and balance — romberg test, tandem walking
    • 
              

              
💡 An experienced neurologist can localize the lesion (exactly WHERE in the nervous system) just from the clinical exam — before any scan.
